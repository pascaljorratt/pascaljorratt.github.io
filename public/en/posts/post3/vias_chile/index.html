Chile, land of poets

  1. 11 de abril de 2026
  2. 2 minutes

📊 View Repository 📊 View Application

I’ll get straight to the point. The poet Stella Díaz Varín was chosen as one of the 10 most prominent figures in the history of La Serena. However, across the entire region, there isn’t a single avenue or street named after her. Just a small alleyway. Believe it or not, the same goes for Jorge Peña Hen, but that’s another story, isn’t it?

In the same competition, Don Francisco de Aguirre was chosen. You may disagree, but at the end of the day, we owe it to him that La Serena is the second oldest city in Chile, having wiped out the indigenous population to refound the city. Today, one of the city’s most important avenues bears his name. Before President González Videla renovated it, it was called La Alameda.

And in the rest of the country? I checked the MINVU database of street names and analysed their frequency. You can search for your favourite figure here. The result? There are no avenues or streets in Chile named after Stella Díaz Varín, just three alleys. If you think it’s no big deal because she didn’t win the National Prize for Literature, bear in mind that Gonzalo Rojas, who won it in 1992, also has just three alleys, with no avenues or streets. And what about my favourite poets? Pardon the intrusion. Jorge Teillier lends his name to seven streets. Nicanor Parra, to eight.

You might say there are more streets named after Gabriela Mistral than after Christopher Columbus. You’re right. However, five avenues are named after the poet and 16 after our favourite conquistador.

Chile is a country of poets with avenues named after conquistadors.
